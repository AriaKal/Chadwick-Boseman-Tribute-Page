Chadwick Boseman Tribute

Chadwick Boseman

1976-2020

Timeline of Chadwick's life

  • Born on November 29, 1976 in Anderson, South Carolina.
  • After his friend was shot and killed, he wrote and directed his first play "Crossroads."
  • Graduated from T. L. Hanna High School in 1995.
  • Graduated with a Bachelor of Fine Arts from Howard University in 2000.
  • Studied acting at the Digital Film Academy in New York.
  • Drama instructor at the Schomburg Junior Scholars Program.
  • First TV appearance as "David Wafer" in "Third Watch."
  • Recurring role as "Reggie Porter" in "All My Children."
  • Appeared in several hit TV shows, namely "ER," "Law & Order," and "CSI:NY."
  • Wrote several plays including "Deep Azure," "Rhyme Deferred," and "Hieroglyphic Graffiti."
  • First lead role portraying Jackie Robinson in "42."
  • Played an NFL draft prospect alongside Kevin Costner in "Draft Day."
  • Started dating singer Taylor Simone Ledward.
  • Portrayed James Brown in "Get On Up."
  • Became part of the Marvel Franchise by portraying the character of T'Challa / Black Panther.
  • Starred in Marvel's Blockbuster "Black Panther," playing T'Challa.
  • Reprised as Black Panther in " Avengers: Infinity War" as well as "Avengers: Endgame."
  • Starred in Netflix's war drama "Da 5 Bloods."
  • Passed away at his home on August 28, 2020 after 4 years of battling colon cancer.
